Six risks your current systems cannot see

Every one of these risks originates in the same place: a contract, a lease, or a piece of legislation that no system in your organisation has read. The transactions flow. The numbers are calculated. But nobody has verified them against the source documents.

1. Regulatory downgrade risk
The RSH is conducting proactive inspections and publishing governance downgrades by name. A downgrade triggers covenant review clauses in loan agreements, Moody's credit reassessment, and board resignations. Ocean Housing was downgraded specifically for weaknesses in procurement, contract management and budgetary control.
RSH Regulatory Judgement, January 2026
2. Tribunal exposure risk
66% of housing associations challenged at tribunal are found to be overcharging on service charges. The root cause is not intent. It is disconnection between the lease that governs the charge and the system that calculates it. Every unvalidated service charge is an unquantified tribunal liability.
SHAC Tribunal Research, February 2026
3. Service charge leakage risk
Housing associations spend £625 million more on services than they recover through service charges each year. The sector is simultaneously overcharging on specific lines and under-recovering on aggregate. The worst possible combination: tribunal risk and financial loss at the same time.
Derived from RSH Global Accounts
4. Legislative non-compliance risk
The Leasehold and Freehold Reform Act 2024 introduces standardised service charge statements and enhanced transparency requirements. STAIRs obligations land in October 2026, giving tenants the right to request information about how charges are calculated. In Wales: the new Rent and Service Charge Standard 2026-2036 introduces enhanced affordability and transparency frameworks. Each new requirement demands financial evidence your current systems cannot produce.
Multiple legislative instruments, 2025-2026
5. Covenant headroom risk
Record repairs spend. Margins at historic lows. The RSH warns that financial headroom and capacity to absorb downside risk is reduced across the sector. Every pound you cannot account for at the contract level is a pound that weakens your covenant position and limits your capacity to invest.
RSH Global Accounts and VFM Report, January-March 2026
6. Institutional knowledge risk
Your service charge calculations were built in spreadsheets by people who have left your organisation. The assumptions in those spreadsheets may or may not reflect the current lease terms. Nobody has checked. When the last person who understood a contract leaves, the knowledge leaves with them.
Patent application WAS-2026-002, Background of the Invention

Why nothing else can do this

Existing service charge software automates arithmetic but requires a human to read the lease and configure the rules.

If the human interpretation is wrong, the software produces formatted wrong answers.

Manual forensic audit reads the lease but costs £50,000 to £150,000 per engagement and does not scale.

The problems reappear in the next charging cycle because the underlying process has not changed.

Strata is the world's first system that reads the lease computationally, extracts executable rules, and computes charges directly from those rules. The gap between what the documents say and what the calculations do is closed permanently.

Reads the documents no other system can read
Every tenancy agreement, lease, service charge policy, apportionment schedule, and regulatory instrument ingested in a single pass. 40 to 200 structured rules extracted per document. Each document read once, rules persisted permanently, applied across every subsequent charging cycle without re-reading.
Extracts executable rules, not summaries
Eligible and ineligible cost categories per tenure type. Apportionment methodologies per charge type per scheme. Sinking fund provisions. Reserve fund contributions. Administrative fee bases. Cap and carry-forward constraints. Section 20 thresholds. Shared ownership staircasing rules. Every rule linked to its source clause.
Resolves multi-tenure schemes correctly
A single block may contain social rent, affordable rent, shared ownership at various staircasing percentages, and outright leasehold units. Each tenure type has different eligible charges, different apportionment rules, and different sinking fund obligations. Strata resolves all of them from the source documents simultaneously.
Detects risk in both directions
Overcharges that expose you to tribunal challenge and reputational damage. Under-recoveries that represent legitimate income you are not collecting. Both identified simultaneously, classified by root cause, referenced to the specific clause where the actual charge diverges from what the lease permits.
Produces tribunal-ready evidence
Every computed charge linked to the source rule, the source clause in the tenancy instrument, and the input data used in the computation. A complete, immutable audit record designed to satisfy the evidentiary requirements of First-Tier Tribunal proceedings.
Deterministic and reproducible
Given the same rules and the same cost data, the engine produces identical results on every execution. Any historical charge calculation can be independently verified. Run this year's charges, last year's charges, and a what-if scenario side by side, each producing a clean, reproducible result.

How it works

Contract to clause. Clause to charge.
Charge to home. Home to law. Proved.

Strata sits above your existing systems. It reads the documents they cannot. It validates every financial transaction against the contract and the law. It produces the home-level audit trail that proof requires.

1
Contract to clause
Every contract, lease, and service charge policy read in a single pass. Every commercial term extracted into a structured knowledge graph: pricing models, scope, SLA targets, payment conditions, apportionment methodologies, sinking fund provisions, tenure-specific rules. Each rule linked to its source clause.
2
Clause to charge
Every invoice, service charge calculation and rent computation matched against the extracted rules. Is this charge within scope? Is this rate current? Is this apportionment consistent with the lease? Is this sinking fund contribution calculated on the correct base? Every deviation flagged. Every compliance point confirmed.
3
Charge to home
Every validated charge allocated to the individual home it serves. Multi-tenure schemes resolved correctly: different eligible charges, different apportionment rules, different sinking fund obligations for different tenure types within the same block. The result: a true financial position for every home in the portfolio.
4
Home to law
Every financial transaction cross-referenced against the applicable legislation. In Wales: the Regulatory Framework, the Rent and Service Charge Standard 2026-2036, the Renting Homes Act. In England: the Landlord and Tenant Act, the Rent Standard, the Leasehold and Freehold Reform Act 2024. A living layer, updated as the law changes.
✓
Proved
A permanent, auditable compliance evidence layer. Not a dashboard on top of unvalidated data. Not a report assembled under pressure before an inspection. A living record that is always current, always auditable, and always ready: for the regulator, the tribunal, the board, and the tenant.
