The value erosion problem

Six ways your contracts leak value after signature

World Commerce & Contracting has tracked contract value erosion for over a decade. Their latest research, published in partnership with Ironclad in January 2026, puts procurement contract leakage at 11%. The causes are structural. They exist in every contract-heavy organisation. They are invisible until someone reads the contracts and checks the numbers.

Rate card drift
You negotiated rates for labour, materials, and services. Those rates have escalation provisions, anniversary dates, and index-linked adjustments. Your AP team processes invoices against the PO. Nobody checks whether the rate on the invoice matches the rate in the contract for that date, that volume, that service category. The supplier knows. You do not.
WorldCC: unauthorised scope changes and missed price adjustments are primary leakage sources
Volume discount erosion
Your contract contains volume-based pricing tiers. At 10,000 units you get a lower rate. At 50,000 units, lower still. But the volume is tracked in your procurement system and the rate is tracked in the supplier's billing system. Nobody reconciles whether the volume threshold was crossed and the discount applied. The larger you are, the more you lose.
SLA penalties uncollected
Your service contracts contain SLA targets with financial penalties for non-performance. Response times. Resolution times. Uptime guarantees. Delivery windows. The SLA is in the contract. The performance data is in your operations system. Nobody connects them. The penalty is your entitlement. If you do not claim it, it does not exist.
Scope creep undetected
The supplier invoices for items or services outside the contracted scope. Emergency call-out rates applied to routine work. Premium materials charged when standard was specified. Out-of-hours rates applied during business hours. Each line item is small. Across hundreds of invoices, it is substantial. Your contract defines what is in scope. Your system does not check.
Auto-renewal on unfavourable terms
Contracts renew automatically unless notice is given within a specific window. The renewal terms may include price increases that exceed your negotiated caps. The notice window is in the contract. Nobody is tracking it systematically. By the time you notice, the window has closed and you are locked in for another cycle at the wrong price.
WorldCC: auto-renewals on unfavourable terms identified as key leakage source, January 2026
Consumables and pass-through markups
Your contract specifies that consumables are passed through at cost, or at cost plus an agreed margin. But "at cost" depends on the supplier's procurement price, which you never see. And the agreed margin differs by category, by contract, by amendment. The markup rules are in the contract. The invoices are in your AP system. The gap between them is your money.

How it works

Contract to clause. Clause to invoice.
Invoice to variance. Variance to recovery.

Strata sits above your existing systems. It reads the contracts they cannot. It validates every financial transaction against the commercial rules those contracts contain. It produces the audit trail that proof requires.

1
Contract to clause
Every contract, framework agreement, SLA schedule, rate card, and amendment read using a combination of machine learning extraction and large language model interpretation. Every commercial term extracted into a structured knowledge graph: pricing tiers, escalation formulas, volume discount thresholds, SLA targets and penalties, scope boundaries, recharge entitlements, pass-through margins, renewal windows. Each rule linked to its source clause.
2
Clause confirmed
Extracted rules are presented to your commercial, procurement, or finance team for review and confirmation. Every rule shown alongside the source clause it was derived from. Your team confirms, adjusts, or rejects. Once confirmed, the rule is frozen into a deterministic logic graph. No AI is involved in the validation step. No hallucination risk. No interpretation drift.
3
Invoice to variance
Every invoice line, every service charge, every consumable, every labour charge matched against the confirmed rules. Is this rate correct for this date? Has the volume threshold been crossed? Is this item within scope? Is this escalation correctly applied? Does this pass-through match the agreed margin? Every deviation flagged with the specific clause reference, the reasoning, and the financial impact.
4
Variance to recovery
Overcharges identified with clause citations ready for dispute or credit note. Under-recoveries identified where you are entitled to recharge, claim SLA penalties, or apply contractual remedies but are not doing so. Each finding is a line item with a source clause reference and a pound value. The evidence package is already assembled.
✓
Proved
A permanent, auditable compliance evidence layer. Not a dashboard on top of unvalidated data. Not an annual contract review assembled under deadline pressure. A living record that is always current, always auditable, and always ready: for the CFO, the auditor, the supplier, and the board.

Why nothing else can do this

Your ERP system processes transactions. Your CLM system stores contracts. Neither connects them. Manual contract review costs £50,000 to £150,000 per engagement and covers a sample, not a portfolio. The problems reappear in the next billing cycle because the underlying process has not changed.

Strata is the world's first system that reads contracts computationally, extracts executable commercial rules, confirms them with your team, and validates every financial transaction deterministically against the confirmed rules. The gap between what the documents say and what the systems charge is closed permanently.

Reads any commercial contract
Service agreements, framework contracts, SLA schedules, rate cards, purchase agreements, lease terms, amendments, variations, side letters. Each document read once. Rules persisted permanently. Applied across every subsequent billing cycle without re-reading.
Extracts executable rules, not summaries
Pricing tiers and volume bands. Escalation formulas and index references. SLA targets and penalty calculations. Scope definitions and exclusions. Pass-through margins and markup caps. Renewal windows and notice periods. Every rule linked to its source clause, its effective date range, and its dependencies.
Handles contract families
A single supplier relationship may involve a master agreement, multiple work orders, a rate card, an SLA schedule, and three amendments. Each document modifies, extends, or overrides provisions in the others. Strata resolves the complete hierarchy and determines the effective rule at any point in time.
Validates services and consumables
Labour rates by grade, by location, by time of day. Material costs against agreed pass-through margins. Equipment charges against contracted rental rates. Consumable volumes against specification allowances. The commercial logic that governs every type of charge in your supplier portfolio.
Deterministic and reproducible
AI is used for extraction. It is not used for validation. Confirmed rules are executable logic. Given the same rules and the same input data, the engine produces identical output on every execution. Any historical validation can be independently verified. Auditor-ready. Board-ready. Dispute-ready.
Updates when contracts change
When a contract is amended, renewed, or varied, Strata detects which provisions have changed, re-extracts only the affected rules, and invalidates dependent logic. No full re-read. No manual reconfiguration. Full version history preserved for any point-in-time audit or dispute.
