Six risks your current systems cannot see

Every one of these risks originates in the same place: a lease, a management agreement, or a piece of legislation that no system in your organisation has read. The transactions flow. The numbers are calculated. But nobody has verified them against the source documents.

Tribunal exposure risk
Over 70% of service charge disputes at the First-tier Tribunal (Property Chamber) result in findings against the landlord. The root cause is not intent. It is disconnection between the lease that governs the charge and the system that calculates it. Every unvalidated service charge is an unquantified tribunal liability.
FTT (Property Chamber) published decisions, 2023-2025
Contract value erosion
Organisations lose an average of 8.6% of contract value through commercial leakage: charges applied incorrectly, entitlements not collected, cost categories allocated without clause authority. Across a portfolio of managed estates, this represents a significant and recurring financial loss that compounds annually.
WorldCC / Deloitte Commercial Performance Study
Legislative change risk
The Leasehold and Freehold Reform Act 2024 introduces standardised service charge demand formats, enhanced transparency obligations, and strengthened leaseholder rights to challenge. These requirements demand financial evidence at a granularity your current processes cannot produce. Non-compliance exposes you to challenge on every demand you issue.
Leasehold and Freehold Reform Act 2024, Part 2
Section 20 procedural risk
Major works above the statutory threshold require formal consultation. A procedural error at any stage caps your recovery to £250 per leaseholder regardless of actual cost. The consultation requirements, the qualifying criteria, and the threshold rules are all governed by lease terms that no system in your organisation reads computationally.
Landlord and Tenant Act 1985, s.20
Multi-lease complexity risk
A single estate may contain residential leases from different eras, commercial leases with different service charge caps, and shared areas with overlapping apportionment obligations. Each lease defines its own scope, its own methodology, and its own exclusions. Your managing agent applies one template. The leases say otherwise.
Common finding in FTT determinations
Institutional knowledge risk
Your service charge calculations were built in spreadsheets by people who may no longer be in post. The assumptions in those spreadsheets may or may not reflect the current lease terms. Nobody has checked. When the last person who understood a particular lease leaves, the knowledge leaves with them.
Patent application WAS-2026-002, Background of the Invention

Why nothing else can do this

Existing service charge software automates arithmetic but requires a human to read the lease and configure the rules. If the human interpretation is wrong, the software produces formatted wrong answers. Manual forensic audit reads the lease but costs £50,000 to £150,000 per engagement and does not scale. The problems reappear in the next charging cycle because the underlying process has not changed.

Strata is the world's first system that reads the lease computationally, extracts executable rules, and validates charges directly against those rules. The gap between what the documents say and what the calculations do is closed permanently.

Reads the documents no other system can read
Every lease, management agreement, service charge schedule, and statutory instrument ingested in a single pass. 40 to 200 structured rules extracted per document. Each document read once, rules persisted permanently, applied across every subsequent charging cycle without re-reading.
Extracts executable rules, not summaries
Eligible and ineligible cost categories per lease. Apportionment methodologies per charge type per estate. Sinking fund provisions. Reserve fund obligations. Management fee bases. Cap and carry-forward constraints. Section 20 thresholds. Sweeper clause boundaries. Every rule linked to its source clause.
Resolves multi-lease estates correctly
A single estate may contain residential leases from the 1970s, modern 999-year leases, commercial ground-floor units, and shared areas with overlapping obligations. Each lease defines its own scope, methodology, and exclusions. Strata resolves all of them from the source documents simultaneously.
Detects risk in both directions
Overcharges that expose you to tribunal challenge and reputational damage. Under-recoveries that represent legitimate income you are not collecting. Both identified simultaneously, classified by root cause, referenced to the specific clause where the actual charge diverges from what the lease permits.
Produces tribunal-ready evidence
Every computed charge linked to the source rule, the source clause in the lease, and the input data used in the computation. A complete, immutable audit record designed to satisfy the evidentiary requirements of First-tier Tribunal (Property Chamber) proceedings.
Deterministic and reproducible
Given the same rules and the same cost data, the engine produces identical results on every execution. Any historical charge calculation can be independently verified. Run this year's charges, last year's charges, and a what-if scenario side by side, each producing a clean, reproducible result.

How it works

Lease to clause. Clause to charge.
Charge to unit. Unit to law. Proved.

Strata sits above your existing systems. It reads the documents they cannot. It validates every financial transaction against the lease and the law. It produces the unit-level audit trail that proof requires.

1
Lease to clause
Every lease, management agreement, and service charge schedule read in a single pass. Every commercial term extracted into a structured knowledge graph: service charge scope, apportionment methodology, reserve fund obligations, cap provisions, management fee bases, sweeper clause boundaries. Each rule linked to its source clause.
2
Clause to charge
Every service charge demand, every cost allocation, and every apportionment calculation matched against the extracted rules. Is this cost within scope? Is this apportionment methodology correct for this lease? Is this reserve fund contribution calculated on the correct base? Does this sweeper clause cover the item? Every deviation flagged. Every compliance point confirmed.
3
Charge to unit
Every validated charge allocated to the individual unit it serves. Multi-lease estates resolved correctly: different lease eras with different eligible charges, different apportionment schedules, different reserve fund obligations for residential and commercial units within the same building. The result: a true financial position for every unit in the portfolio.
4
Unit to law
Every financial transaction cross-referenced against the applicable legislation. The Landlord and Tenant Act 1985. The Commonhold and Leasehold Reform Act 2002. The Leasehold and Freehold Reform Act 2024. Section 20 consultation requirements. STAIRs transparency obligations. A living layer, updated as the law changes.
✓
Proved
A permanent, auditable compliance evidence layer. Not a dashboard on top of unvalidated data. Not a report assembled under pressure before a tribunal hearing. A living record that is always current, always auditable, and always ready: for the tribunal, the leaseholder, the auditor, and the board.
